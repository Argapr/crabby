About Crabby

Crabs are marine animals that belong to the Crustacea family and have a hard shell that serves as a body protector. The animal has five pairs of legs, with one pair of them developing into powerful claws to catch food and survive predators. Crabs can be found in a variety of habitats, including seas, rivers, and beaches. They generally live at the bottom of the waters, digging in sand or mud for shelter.
